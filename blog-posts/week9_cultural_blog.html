C9:Asking the Right Questions

10/03/2014

You can ask someone what kind of music they like, but you're more likely to get an accurate answer if you ask what type of music they've been listening to lately.

I’m pretty sure the motto for my middle school was “There’s no such thing as a dumb question.” It’s interesting that we’ve been taught to ask questions, but not actually how to ask questions. By asking poorly worded and structured questions, we’re less likely to get an accurate or helpful response. In some cases, the correct answer might not really matter in the grand scheme of things. However that won’t always be the case, and if you’ve ever spent a late night googling without getting any helpful Stack Overflow articles then knowing how to ask questions can be a huge time saver.

The worst is when you’re reading a headline and it’s too vague to mean anything. Be clear about what your question is, and ensure that everything is spelled right. If nothing else, make sure that the question is phrased in a way that makes sense.

Be specific about what your problem is and supply as much relevant detail as possible in your question. Make it as easy as possible for someone to help you. And for god’s sake, if your questions is regarding code, put in the code that you’re having a problem with! It removes a lot of guess work for the people who will be answering the question, which means that it becomes more likely that someone will answer your question.

Finally, reply to the answers that you’ve received. Did they work? If not, what happened when you tried the solution? Make a point to update about your situation - not only is doing so polite, but will be appreciated when (inevitably) someone else has the same question.
